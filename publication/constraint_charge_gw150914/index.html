General relativistic simulations of the quasi-circular inspiral and merger of charged black holes: GW150914 and fundamental physics implications

Gabriele Bozzola, Vasileios Paschalidis
June 2020
PDF

Abstract

We perform general-relativistic simulations of charged black holes targeting GW150914. We show that the early inspiral is most efficient for detecting black hole charge through gravitational waves and that GW150914 places only weak constraints on the charge-to-mass ratio, $Q/ M< 0.4$. Our work applies to electric and magnetic charge, and to theories with black holes endowed with U(1) (hidden or dark) charges. With our results, we place an upper bound on the deviation of the so-called theory of MOdified Gravity (MOG) from general relativity in the dynamical, strong-field regime.
